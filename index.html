Biography

I am currently a Ph.D. student in the Department of Mechanical and Automation Engineering, The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), supervised by Prof. Yunjian Xu. Before that, I received the B. Eng. degree from the School of Electrical Engineering and Automation, Wuhan University (WHU) in 2016. After graduation, I worked as a research assistant at the Center for Grid Power Electronics (CGPES) for one year.

My research interests cover stochastic dynamic programming and deep reinforcement learning, with their applications in smart grid. Specifically, I am interested in designing dynamic scheduling framework for electric vehicles charging and parking stations with consideration of multiple uncertainties to, such as random arrival and dwell time of vehicles, stochastic utility price in electricity markets and intermittent renewable generation.
